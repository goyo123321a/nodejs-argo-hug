About New York City

New York City, often called simply New York, is the most populous city in the United States. With an estimated 2023 population of 8.8 million people distributed over 300.46 square miles (778.2 kmÂ²), New York City is also the most densely populated major city in the United States.

Located at the southern tip of the state of New York, the city is the center of the New York metropolitan area, the largest metropolitan area in the world by urban area. NYC is composed of five boroughs: Brooklyn, Queens, Manhattan, The Bronx, and Staten Island.

New York City has been described as the cultural, financial, and media capital of the world, and exerts a significant impact upon commerce, entertainment, research, technology, education, politics, tourism, art, fashion, and sports.

Iconic Landmarks

Statue of Liberty

A symbol of freedom and democracy, this iconic statue was a gift from France in 1886. Located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, it welcomes visitors from around the world.

Times Square

Known as "The Crossroads of the World," Times Square is famous for its bright billboards, Broadway theaters, and the annual New Year's Eve ball drop watched by millions worldwide.

Central Park

An urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan, Central Park spans 843 acres and features lakes, meadows, playgrounds, the Central Park Zoo, and numerous cultural attractions.

Culture & Lifestyle

World-Class Arts & Theater

New York City is the undisputed theater capital of the world, with Broadway at its heart. The Theater District in Midtown Manhattan features more than 40 professional theaters, each with 500 or more seats.

The city is also home to prestigious institutions like the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), the Guggenheim Museum, and the Whitney Museum of American Art.

Global Culinary Capital

NYC offers one of the world's most diverse culinary landscapes, with over 27,000 restaurants serving cuisine from virtually every country. From street food to Michelin-starred dining, there's something for every palate.

Don't miss classic NYC foods: New York-style pizza, bagels with lox, hot dogs from street vendors, and cheesecake from iconic establishments like Junior's.

Sports City

New York City is home to numerous professional sports teams across all major leagues. The city hosts two NFL teams (Giants and Jets), two MLB teams (Yankees and Mets), two NBA teams (Knicks and Nets), and three NHL teams (Rangers, Islanders, and Devils).

The annual US Open tennis tournament, New York City Marathon, and the Millrose Games track meet are just a few of the major sporting events held in the city each year.

Music & Nightlife

From jazz clubs in Harlem to punk rock in the East Village, NYC's music scene is legendary. Iconic venues like Carnegie Hall, Madison Square Garden, the Apollo Theater, and the Blue Note Jazz Club host world-class performances nightly.

The city's nightlife is equally diverse, with rooftop bars offering spectacular skyline views, underground clubs in Brooklyn, and upscale lounges in Manhattan.

The Five Boroughs

Manhattan

The heart of NYC, featuring Times Square, Central Park, and Wall Street.

Brooklyn

Known for its creative culture, diverse neighborhoods, and iconic bridge.

Queens

The most ethnically diverse urban area in the world, with over 130 languages spoken.

The Bronx

Home to the New York Yankees, the Bronx Zoo, and the birthplace of hip-hop.

Staten Island

Known for the Staten Island Ferry and the Freshkills Park, once the world's largest landfill.

Getting Around NYC

New York City has one of the most extensive public transportation systems in the world. The subway operates 24/7, with buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services readily available throughout the five boroughs.

Best Time to Visit

The best times to visit New York City are from April to June and September to early November when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, each season offers unique experiences - from holiday decorations in winter to outdoor festivals in summer.
